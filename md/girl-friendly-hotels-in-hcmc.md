In general, most hotels in tourist areas are girl-friendly. These include the area around Bùi Viện, the Nguyễn Huệ Walking Street, Japan Town as well as Phú Mỹ Hung (popular with Koreans). 

In any case, it's best to first check back with the hotel to inquire about their guest policy. Many hotels, unfortunately, charge an extra fee for additional guests. Also, always make your booking for two people from the beginning.

Should a hotel somehow give you trouble, there is not much you can do. Note that all guests must be registered by law. You can try to circumvent this. Some big hotels are so busy that no-one can check who comes and goes. Other hotels have elevators that go from underground parking lot straight to the upper floors. This, however, isn't necessarily recommended. Police checks are rare, but can happen (especially before Tet holiday).

If you want to avoid the hassle altogether, book an AirBnB or a serviced apartment. Another alternative is love hotels that allow you to rent rooms by the hour. An example is <a href="https://a25hotel.com" rel="nofollow">A25 Hotel</a>.
